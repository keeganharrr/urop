System Types:

Individual Ground Source Heat Pump (GSHP) A single building's heating/cooling system that uses the stable underground temperature. Pipes buried in the ground exchange heat with the earth.
4th Generation District Heating & Cooling (4GDHC) A shared network where a central plant heats or cools water, then distributes it to multiple buildings through underground pipes. More efficient than individual systems.
5th Generation District Heating & Cooling (5GDHC) The most advanced system: buildings share a common underground water loop and exchange heat with each other. When one building needs cooling, its excess heat can warm another building.

Key Terms Explained:

Heat Pump
A device that moves heat from one place to another (like a refrigerator in reverse). It can both heat and cool buildings using much less energy than traditional furnaces or AC units.
Borehole
A narrow hole drilled into the ground (typically 200-500 feet deep) where pipes are installed to exchange heat with the earth.
Thermal Resources
The underground heat source or sink - the earth maintains a stable temperature year-round, making it an efficient source for heating in winter and cooling in summer.
Distribution Network
The system of underground pipes that carries heated or cooled water between the central system and connected buildings.
